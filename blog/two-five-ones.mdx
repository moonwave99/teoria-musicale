Two-Five-Ones are the foundation of **jazz harmony**, but can be found in any other genre of course.

<Truncate/>

Let's play a `ii/V/I` in `C`:

<Sequence>
  <Part notes="D3 F3 A3 C4" label="ii (Dm7)" articulation="arpeggio"/>
  <Part notes="G3 B3 D4 F4" label="V (G7)" articulation="arpeggio"/>
  <Part notes="C3 E3 G3 B3" label="I (CMaj7)" articulation="rake"/>
</Sequence>

The voicings are very clumsy! Let's arrange them a bit better:

<Sequence>
  <Part notes="D2 A2, F3 C4" label="ii (Dm7)"/>
  <Part notes="G2 D3, B3 F4" label="V (G7)"/>
  <Part notes="C3 G3, B3 E4" label="I (CMaj7)"/>
</Sequence>  

The left hand is not particularly interesting, it's just parallel fifths!

Let's try instead:

<Sequence articulation="rake">
  <Part notes="D2, F3 A3 C4 E4" label="ii (Dm7)"/>
  <Part notes="G2, F3 A3 B3 E4" label="V (G7)"/>
  <Part notes="C3, E3 A3 B3 D4" label="I (CMaj7)"/>
</Sequence>
